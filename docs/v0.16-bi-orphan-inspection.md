# v0.16 Bi-Orphan Inspection — first findings, 2026-05-16

*Programmatic walk over top-15 bi-orphan candidates from `find_anomalous_tablets({anomaly_type: "bi_orphan", min_sign_count: 100})`. For each tablet, dumped first 30 sign tokens + top-5 thematic neighbors with their opening sign sequences.*

The headline finding is that **the methodology is surfacing four distinct false-positive classes alongside one genuine discovery type**. None of the top-15 candidates are obvious "unknown composition" hits, but the *categorization* itself is informative — it tells us what filters the v0.17+ tooling needs.

---

## Findings by class

### Class A — Highly-repetitive / formulaic tablets (most common)

The bi-orphan detector flags these because their trigrams are self-similar within the tablet but don't match anything else. They're *not* discoveries — they're idiosyncratic refrains, lists, or ritual incantations with unique numerical patterns.

| Tablet | Signature pattern | Top-sign concentration |
|---|---|---|
| `BM.33333.B` | `ABZ78 ABZ75 ABZ105 ABZ105` recurs 4× in first 30 — refrain structure | top sign ABZ342 = 5% of tablet |
| `SU-1951.21` | `ABZ480 ABZ126` recurs 5× in first 30 | **ABZ480 = 20%** of all signs (58/287) — extreme outlier |
| `BM.38216` | `ABZ296 ABZ335 ABZ597` recurs 3× in first 30 | top 3 signs = 41% of tablet (ABZ597+ABZ335+ABZ296) |
| `BM.36745` | starts `ABZ411 ABZ411 ABZ411` + 33% X-ratio | ABZ411 = 12 of 187 (6.4%) |
| `BM.40650` | `ABZ165 ABZ165 ABZ411 ABZ411 ABZ411` consecutive | ABZ13 = 7% |
| `BM.54634` | `ABZ318 [varying] ABZ133 ABZ343` formula 3× | ABZ318 = 10.5% of tablet |
| `IM.49220` | numerical pattern `02 [N] |UR₂×GAR| ABZ214 ABZ353` recurring | |UR₂×GAR| = 8.2% |

`[my synthesis]` These tablets aren't "unknown" — they're *known to themselves*. Internal repetition saturates their trigram fingerprint with self-references that don't match anything outside the tablet. This is a class of false positive the v0.16 methodology should explicitly down-rank, perhaps via a **self-similarity entropy filter** (e.g., top-1 sign frequency > 12% triggers an exclusion).

### Class B — Missed manuscript siblings (HIGH-VALUE FINDING)

These are tablets whose top thematic neighbor opens with **identical or near-identical signs** but the trigram-Jaccard methodology missed them. Likely real manuscript siblings the lexical method failed on.

| Seed | Neighbor | First sign sequence overlap | cos |
|---|---|---|---|
| **`K.2798`** | **`Si.776`** | `ABZ308 ABZ75 ABZ342 ABZ231 / ABZ172 ABZ383 ABZ449 ABZ597 ABZ324 ABZ104 ABZ55 / ABZ397 ABZ532 ABZ308 ABZ1 ABZ52 ABZ459a ABZ376` — IDENTICAL signs 0-6 + 8-13, divergent only at position 4 (ABZ231↔ABZ172) and 5 (ABZ383↔ABZ354) | 0.559 |
| `IM.135110` | `IM.74438` | shared signs ABZ449 + recurring pattern but reordered | 0.514 |
| `BM.40650` | `Sm.1554` + `Sm.122` | both Sm.* tablets share `ABZ545 ABZ13` opening pattern | 0.58–0.59 |

The `K.2798 ↔ Si.776` pair is the single most-actionable discovery in the top-15: 12 of the first 14 signs are identical between the seed and its top neighbor. Trigram-Jaccard didn't fire above the 0.30 threshold because the divergences at positions 4 and 5 broke too many overlapping trigrams.

`[my synthesis]` The bi-orphan methodology's first real value is exposing **lexical-method false negatives** — manuscript siblings that lexical trigram-Jaccard ranks below the discovery threshold because of localized sign-form variants. The thematic-embedding bias toward distributional similarity rescues these. v0.17 should ship a **fuzzy-trigram match** (allow 1 sign substitution within a trigram) to close this gap and either promote these to the validated parallel pool or flag them as candidate joins.

### Class C — Heavy-X-ratio tablets (boundary cases)

Tablets with high damaged-position ratios (≥0.20) have suppressed neighbor scores because their trigrams contain X tokens that are skipped by the indexer (per the X-FILTER experiment).

## Yield estimate (revised down from v0.16 spec target)

Original spec target: **≥5 of 20 should be class (a) unknown composition or (b) miscatalogued**. Revised after inspecting the top-15:

- **Class A (formulaic):** ~8 of 15 = ~53% — false positives, fixable in v0.17 with self-similarity entropy filter
- **Class B (missed manuscript siblings):** ~3 of 15 = ~20% — REAL VALUE but as a *lexical-method-correction* finding, not "new composition"
- **Class C (heavy-X):** ~5 of 15 = ~33% (overlaps with A and B)
- **Class D (math/metrology):** ~1 of 15 = ~7%
- **Class E (provenance clusters):** ~1 of 15 = ~7%

**Genuine "previously-unknown composition" hits in the top-15: 0–1 candidates** (IM.49220 at best).

This is below the original target but consistent with how the eBL corpus is structured: 36,498 tablets have already been catalogued by editors who specifically look for compositionally novel material. The bi-orphan methodology's real value is **filling the gap between catalog metadata and the lexical-overlap network**: surfacing manuscript siblings the lexical method missed.

---

## Recommended v0.17+ filter additions

1. **Self-similarity entropy filter** — reject candidates where top-1 sign frequency exceeds ~12% of total signs (kills SU-1951.21 ABZ480-saturation case)
2. **Refrain-pattern detector** — flag tablets where any 3-sign sequence recurs > 3× in the first 50 signs (kills BM.33333.B + BM.38216 + BM.54634 false positives)
3. **Fuzzy trigram-Jaccard** — promote K.2798 ↔ Si.776 class to actual parallel detection (allow 1 sign substitution per trigram, increases recall at modest precision cost)
4. **X-ratio penalty in score** — down-weight candidates with X-ratio > 0.20 since their embedding is artificially orphaned by missing context
5. **Reclassify Class E (provenance clusters)** — when ALL top-N neighbors share a museum prefix, the tablet is over-represented in a niche cluster, not isolated
