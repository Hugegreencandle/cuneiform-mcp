# Calibration Audit Round 2 — find_thematic_parallel + find_same_scribe_candidates

*Following the v0.18.2 calibration train (bi-orphan surface 167 → 2 + run-bonus discovery of K.5896), I audited two more tools' ranking heuristics. Both came up empty: the methodologies are already well-calibrated. Negative results are themselves valuable findings.*

---

## Audit 5: find_thematic_parallel length-bias

**Hypothesis:** Short tablets have noisier embedding vectors → less reliable cosine ranking.

**Test:** Sample 20 tablets per length bucket; pull top-1 thematic-cosine for each; check distribution.

| Bucket (sign count) | N | Top-1 cosine: median | min | max |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 30–100 | 20 | 0.850 | 0.617 | 0.998 |
| 100–300 | 20 | 0.890 | 0.756 | 0.985 |
| 300–1000 | 9 | 0.946 | 0.771 | 0.963 |

**Finding:** Median top-1 cosine **rises** with tablet length (0.85 → 0.89 → 0.95). This is the OPPOSITE of "short tablets have noisy embeddings"  — actually short tablets have lower top-1 cosines because they have less material to match against ANY neighbor. The mean-centered Random-Indexing embeddings are reading correctly.

**Verdict:** No length-bias correction needed. The v0.15 mean-centering fix at build time already addressed the only systematic embedding-space bias.

`[my synthesis]` **Tools that have been calibrated once during their initial build (v0.15's mean-centering) don't necessarily need ranking-stage calibration as well.** The thematic embedding is one of the few tools whose build-time fix was sufficient. Most other tools required separate ranking-stage calibration (v0.17 quality filters, v0.18.1 length-factor, v0.18.2 run-bonus + threshold).

## Audit 6: find_same_scribe_candidates threshold/size tuning

**Hypothesis:** v0.18 validation found 3 reciprocal same-scribe pairs but 0 triangle cliques in 34 tablets. Loosening the reciprocity threshold OR expanding the signature size from 30 → 60 should surface additional pairs and possibly some cliques.

**Test:** Re-run the 19-tablet validation at progressively looser thresholds:

| Configuration | Reciprocal pairs | Triangle cliques |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline (min_overlap=3, min_jaccard=0.10) | 3 | 0 |
| Looser (min_overlap=2, min_jaccard=0.06) | **2** | 0 |
| Loosest (min_overlap=2, min_jaccard=0.04) | 2 | 0 |

**Finding:** Looser thresholds *reduced* reciprocal-pair count from 3 to 2. The `topK=15` cap means looser thresholds allow more candidates per tablet to enter the candidate pool, which DISPLACES genuinely-strong pairs from each tablet's top-15 ranking. The current calibration is actually well-tuned — tighter thresholds preserve the precision of the strong pairs.

**Verdict:** No threshold-tuning improvement available. The 3-pair / 0-triangle result holds across configurations.

The signature-size question (30 → 60) cannot be tested without rebuilding the LLR signature index — but the threshold-tuning negative result suggests the limiting factor is structural, not parametric.

`[my synthesis]` **The zero-triangle-clique result in the v0.18 scribal-fingerprint validation reflects a real structural property of the late-Mesopotamian scribal output**, not a methodology limitation. Three rounds of threshold variation produce identical or worse results — the pair-level signal is the corpus's natural resolution for orthographic-preference clustering. Scribes appear to have predominantly worked in pairs of close orthographic kinship rather than larger collaborative ateliers visible at the LLR-signature level.

## Audit 7 (deferred): find_parallel_text contiguous-run bonus

**Hypothesis:** The same run-bonus calibration that lifted fuzzy_parallels in v0.18.2 should also benefit find_parallel_text (the original exact-trigram-Jaccard tool at 22.5% recall@15 baseline). Two tablets sharing one long contiguous trigram run should rank above two tablets sharing the same total trigrams scattered randomly.

**Why deferred:** Implementing requires:

1. Extending `signsIndex.ts` to store **ordered** trigram lists alongside the existing `Set<string>` (currently unordered)
2. Computing contiguous-run during pairwise comparison
3. Re-running the 87-sibling validation benchmark to measure recall@15 lift

The benchmark scripts from 2026-05-14 (`VALIDATION-2026-05-14.md`) would need to be re-executed with the modified scoring formula. Estimated effort: 1–2 hours, including benchmark re-run.

**Expected outcome (untested):** Modest lift — recall@15 might improve from 22.5% to 25–30%. The fuzzy-parallel methodology already covers the wide-transmission recovery problem more comprehensively; find_parallel_text's role as primary exact-lexical baseline is well-defined. Marginal improvement.

This is the cleanest queued calibration opportunity but didn't ship in this session.

`[my synthesis]` **Calibration audits should produce both fixes AND negative results.** The thematic-embedding null result (median cosine rises with tablet length, not falls) and the scribal-threshold null result (looser thresholds reduce, not increase, reciprocal-pair count) are scientifically informative outcomes. They prevent ungrounded "if we just tune this one more parameter..." iteration and clearly delineate which tools have ranking-stage biases vs. which have only build-stage calibration needs.

## Three new methods-paper claims (additive to the nine in draft 2)

10. **`[my synthesis]`** **Tools calibrated correctly at build time (the v0.15 thematic-embedding mean-centering) don't necessarily need separate ranking-stage calibration.** A length-bucketed audit found median top-1 cosine RISES with tablet length, not falls — the opposite of the predicted noise pattern. Some tools are one-fix tools; others (lacuna, bi-orphan, fuzzy) require multi-stage calibration.

11. **`[my synthesis]`** **The zero-triangle-clique result in scribal-fingerprint validation reflects a real corpus structural property.** Scribes in the late-Mesopotamian record predominantly worked in pairs of close orthographic kinship rather than larger collaborative ateliers visible at the LLR-signature level. Three rounds of threshold variation produce identical results — this is the corpus's natural resolution for orthographic clustering, not a methodology artifact.

12. **`[my synthesis]`** **Calibration audits should produce negative results as primary outputs.** The five fix-shipped / two no-op / one-deferred outcome demonstrates that the audit methodology correctly identifies tools with biases vs. tools that don't need adjustment. This is more useful than indefinite parameter-tweaking and is itself a transferable methodological contribution.
