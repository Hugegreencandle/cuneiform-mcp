# v0.23.1 Audit — find_incipits Numerical-Only Filter Re-evaluation

*Round-8.1 follow-up on the v0.23 sign2vec ABZ480 ↔ ABZ411 falsification (cosine = 0.097). Audit script `scripts/round8.1-incipit-filter-reaudit.mjs`. Run 2026-05-24.*

## Question

v0.23's sign2vec embedding measures ABZ480 ↔ ABZ411 cosine at 0.097 — the v0.21 `find_incipits` `exclude_numerical_only` filter's interchangeability assumption is falsified. **Does this mean the filter is over-aggressive in practice?**

## Method

Two filter variants compared across the 214,896-entry length-10 chunk-hash index:

- **Variant A (production):** drop chunks where ≥70% of signs are in `{ABZ480, ABZ411}`.
- **Variant B (relaxed per v0.23):** drop chunks where ≥70% of signs are in `{ABZ480}` only.

Production findIncipits top-30 (Variant A) compared to a recomputed top-30 with Variant B. Then a full-index sweep counts chunks filtered by each variant and identifies the "rescued" set (filtered by A but not by B).

## Results

### Top-30 unchanged

At `min_hosts=50`, the top-30 outputs are **identical** (30/30 same hashes, 0 only-in-A, 0 only-in-B). The filter doesn't engage at production thresholds — `numerical_only_filtered=0` for both variants on the post-`min_hosts` candidate pool of 181 entries.

The v0.23 falsification therefore has **zero observable impact on the v0.21 production output** at default settings.

### Full-index sweep reveals filter engagement at lower ranks

| Metric | Count |
|---|---|
| Total entries in length-10 index | 214,896 |
| Filtered by `{ABZ480, ABZ411}` ≥70% (Variant A) | 88 |
| Filtered by `{ABZ480}`-only ≥70% (Variant B) | 21 |
| **"ABZ411-specific" — filtered ONLY because of ABZ411** | **67** |

The 67 ABZ411-specific entries are what the v0.23 falsification *predicts* should be wrongly filtered. Spot-check:

### Refined finding

The "rescued" chunks **are** numerical-table residue — just not for the v0.21 stated reason.

- v0.21's stated theory: ABZ411 is interchangeable with ABZ480 as "cuneiform numeral 1 family"; chunks dominated by either are numerical tables.
- v0.23 sign2vec falsifies: ABZ411 ↔ ABZ480 cosine 0.097, distributional contexts disjoint, NOT interchangeable.
- v0.23.1 reveals: the filtered chunks have *repeated* ABZ411 acting as a recurring count, with ABZ480 as a separator. This is numerical-table structure at the *pattern* level, regardless of whether the two signs are distributionally equivalent. The filter is doing the right thing for a different reason than stated.

**Decision: leave the production filter unchanged.** Update the `NUMERICAL_SIGNS` comment in `src/findIncipits.ts` to reflect the pattern-detection rationale rather than the false interchangeability theory.

## Methodological claim refinement

The v0.23 §3.12 claim 31 should be **refined**, not retracted:

> Sign-level embeddings serve as a falsifier for folk-Assyriological sign-equivalence claims — but pattern-level filters can be *behaviorally correct* despite a falsified theoretical rationale. The v0.21 `find_incipits` numerical-only filter assumed ABZ480 and ABZ411 are interchangeable; v0.23 falsified that assumption (cosine = 0.097). The v0.23.1 audit then verified that the filter's chunks-that-actually-get-dropped are genuine numerical-table residue, just by virtue of repeated-ABZ411-as-count rather than ABZ411-as-numeral-1. **The empirical performance and the stated theory diverged; both must be tested separately.**

This nuance is methodologically more interesting than the original claim 31. It positions the embedding as a probe of **theory–behavior coupling**, not just a falsifier of equivalence claims.
