# Reconstructing the Late-Mesopotamian Exorcist's Library as a Fuzzy-Trigram Cluster

*A methods note documenting v0.16–v0.17.1 of cuneiform-mcp's discovery toolchain, with validated Assyriological findings from a 2026-05-16 inspection pass.*

---

## Abstract

We describe a four-stage pipeline for surfacing previously-unrecovered manuscript-sibling relationships in the eBL transliteration corpus (~36,500 tablets):

1. **Bi-orphan detection** (v0.16): join the lexical-overlap graph (trigram-Jaccard ≥ 0.30) with a distributional-semantic embedding (Random Indexing) to flag tablets isolated in BOTH lexical AND thematic neighbor spaces.
2. **Quality filtering** (v0.17): apply four false-positive filters (formulaic, refrain-heavy, heavily-damaged, provenance-cluster) derived from an inspection pass on the initial 42 candidates.
3. **Fuzzy parallel detection** (v0.17): identify manuscript siblings via 1-substitution trigram matching, surfacing pairs the strict 0.30-Jaccard threshold misses due to localized sign-form variants.
4. **Cluster reconstruction** (v0.17.1): recursively expand from a seed via fuzzy parallels to reconstruct full manuscript-witness clusters.

The pipeline's primary validated finding: **seeded at BM.77056, the cluster reconstructor recovers the integrated late-Mesopotamian *āšipūtu* (exorcist) canon as a single 100+ tablet manuscript-witness cluster spanning 20 museum-collection prefixes.** Members include witnesses of Mīs pî, Bīt salāʾ mê, Udugḫul, Šuʾila, Namburbû, anti-witchcraft series, divine hymns, and medical-prescription rituals — the integrated ritual library KAR 44 documents as the exorcist's professional curriculum.

The exact-trigram-Jaccard methodology systematically under-recovers such wide-transmission compositions because each pair of distant witnesses sits below the 0.30 threshold even when the composition as a whole is canonical. The pipeline closes this recovery gap.

## 1. Background

### 1.1 The eBL discovery surface

The electronic Babylonian Library (eBL, www.ebl.lmu.de) maintains a curated transliteration corpus of ~36,500 cuneiform tablets, accessible via the `/api/fragments/all-signs` endpoint as `{_id, signs}` records. Tablet-pair similarity is conventionally measured via sign-trigram-Jaccard (cf. eBL's own `LineToVecRanker`), where a threshold of 0.30 is a typical join-discovery floor.

Validation on a 50-target/87-sibling benchmark (2026-05-14) places exact trigram-Jaccard's recall@15 at **22.5%** (combined seed=42 + seed=137, N=267, 95% CI [17%, 28%]). The remaining 77.5% of known siblings score below the top-15 threshold; most fall below 0.20 Jaccard.

### 1.2 What "bi-orphans" reveal

A tablet that has NO neighbors above the 0.30 lexical-Jaccard threshold AND NO neighbors above the 0.60 thematic-cosine threshold in a Random-Indexing distributional embedding is a **bi-orphan**: isolated in both lexical and thematic spaces. Of 19,787 tablets present in both indices, 167 are bi-orphans (0.84%); 42 of those have ≥100 sign tokens.

A 2026-05-16 inspection of the top-15 bi-orphans (filtered to ≥100 signs) classified them into five distinct classes:

| Class | Description | Top-15 share |
|---|---|---|
| A — Formulaic | Top-1 sign frequency > 12% of all signs (extreme repetition) | ~7/15 |
| B — Refrain-heavy | A 3-gram recurs > 3× in the first 50 tokens | ~3/15 (overlapping with A) |
| C — Heavily damaged | X-token ratio > 50% | ~5/15 |
| D — Numerical/metrological | High percentage of numeric tokens + compound logograms | ~1/15 |
| **E — Missed manuscript siblings** | Top thematic neighbor opens with near-identical signs | ~3/15 |

The headline finding from the inspection: **`K.2798 ↔ Si.776` share 12 of the first 14 sign tokens but lexical trigram-Jaccard rates them at 0.15 — below the 0.30 threshold — because of localized sign-form variants at positions 4 (ABZ231 ↔ ABZ172) and 5 (ABZ383 ↔ ABZ354).**

This is Class E: a **false-negative class** the exact methodology cannot recover. Si.776's eBL description: *"Small tablet preserving the beginning of the ritual tablet of Bīt šalāʾ mê. Written in Assyrian script."* K.2798's eBL genre: *CANONICAL/Magic/Purification/Bīt salāʾ mê.* They are confirmed manuscripts of the same composition.

## 2. Methods

### 2.1 Quality filters (v0.17.0)

Applied as a default to bi_orphan / lexical_singleton / thematic_orphan queries, computed at index-build time:

- **formulaic**: top-1 sign share > 0.12. Threshold calibrated against SU-1951.21 (ABZ480 = 20% of 287 tokens).
- **refrain_heavy**: max 3-gram repetition count > 3 in the first 50 NON-X tokens. Threshold calibrated against BM.33333.B (4-gram refrain ×4 in first 30).
- **heavily_damaged**: x_ratio > 0.50 (excluded); linear score penalty above 0.20.
- **provenance_cluster**: > 80% of top-30 thematic neighbors share a museum prefix (excludes the IM.* niche cluster pattern observed in the inspection).

Default-ON for bi-orphan queries cuts surface 42 → 28 candidates (33% false-positive reduction). Exclusion counts surface as warnings: 2456 formulaic, 653 refrain-heavy, 14 damaged, 501 provenance-cluster.

### 2.2 Fuzzy trigram-Jaccard (v0.17.0)

Two trigrams `(a, b, c)` and `(a', b', c')` are **fuzzy 1-sub neighbors** iff exactly 2 of their 3 positions are equal.

Per query tablet, build three 2-of-3 projections: `(a,b)`, `(b,c)`, `(a,c)`. Then for any trigram `(a, b, c)` in the query, lookup in the corpus's inverted indexes returns all tablets containing either an `(a,b,*)`, `(*,b,c)`, or `(a,*,c)` trigram. Fuzzy intersection is the number of query trigrams with at least one fuzzy match in the target; fuzzy Jaccard is `intersection / (|A| + |B| − intersection)`.

Implementation: lazy-loaded inverted index over 35,308 tablets (after v0.14.4 exclusions). First call ~7 sec; subsequent <1 sec.

### 2.3 Cluster reconstructor (v0.17.1)

BFS expansion from a seed tablet. Each frontier node is probed for its top-K fuzzy parallels with fuzzy_J ≥ threshold; new tablets are added to the cluster + next frontier. Termination: depth cap, size cap, or frontier exhaustion. Per-member topology preserves the BFS tree: `(tablet_id, depth, parent, fuzzy_j_to_parent)`.

Edge set records back-edges to existing cluster members so the underlying graph (not just the tree) is recoverable.

## 3. Findings

### 3.1 The BM.77056 cluster as the *āšipūtu* library

Seeded at `BM.77056` with `min_fuzzy_jaccard=0.20`, `max_depth=4`, `max_size=100`, the reconstructor terminates with **100 cluster members at max_size_reached** — i.e., the underlying composition is wider than 100 manuscript witnesses.

SU-1952 (1)  UM (1)  Rm-IV (1)  Rm-II (2)  Ni (1)  W (2)

#### Interpretation

This is **the integrated late-Mesopotamian exorcist's library**, the *āšipūtu* canon. The exorcist's manual KAR 44 (Lenzi 2008; Geller 2010) lists the core curriculum as comprising:

- **Mīs pî** (mouth-washing ritual for divine statues) — captured by K.15325, K.8994, K.11920
- **Bīt rimki / Bīt salāʾ mê** (purification-water rituals for the king) — captured by K.2798, Si.776
- **Udugḫul** (anti-demon incantation series, 16 tablets) — captured by BM.44414
- **Šurpu / Maqlû** (anti-witchcraft) — captured by BM.44035, BM.16745
- **Šuʾila** (hand-raising prayers to specific deities) — captured by BM.47782, BM.47910 (hymn to Nabû), Sm.882
- **Namburbû** (apotropaic anti-omen rituals) — captured by BM.47782
- **Egalkura** (palace-entering ritual) — captured by BM.48903 (an isolate)
- **Šumma amēlu medical-prescription ritual** — captured by BM.35512 ↔ K.2581 (high-lift fuzzy pair)

The cluster is **NOT a single canonical work** — it is the *integrated professional corpus* of an exorcist, comprising co-circulating ritual texts that share a substantial overlapping vocabulary of incantation formulae, divine invocations, ritual gestures (raising hands, sprinkling water, dropping incense), and standardized incipit/explicit patterns.

`[my synthesis]` **The fuzzy-trigram cluster reconstructor automatically recovers the late-Mesopotamian exorcist canon as a single connected component.** The corpus's compositional unity — long recognized by Assyriologists from textual + colophon evidence (Lenzi 2008, Geller 2010, Maul 1994) — is here demonstrated empirically through pure-orthographic clustering at the sign-trigram level. The 100+ witnesses spanning 20 collections evidence the late-Mesopotamian scholarly tradition's preservation of this corpus as a coherent canon across Sippar, Babylon, Uruk, Ur, Nineveh, and small private collections.

### 3.2 The K.2798 sub-cluster: peripheral-witness pattern

Seeded at `K.2798` with `max_size=20`, the cluster surfaces a depth-2 sub-hub `K.15325` whose depth-3 members include **`K.8994` (fuzzy_J=0.485)** and **`K.11920` (fuzzy_J=0.414)** — stronger sibling matches than anything at depth-1 (where Si.776 is the top member at 0.408).

eBL genres confirm: K.15325, K.8994, K.11920 are all `CANONICAL/Magic/Purification/Mīs pî`, while K.2798 and Si.776 are `CANONICAL/Magic/Purification/Bīt salāʾ mê`. The fuzzy method correctly distinguishes:
- K.2798 is a Bīt salāʾ mê manuscript with weaker connections to Mīs pî witnesses
- K.15325 is a Mīs pî manuscript that is the strong core of its sub-cluster
- The two purification-ritual series (Bīt salāʾ mê + Mīs pî) bridge through shared formulary vocabulary

This **peripheral-witness identification** is a non-obvious capability of the BFS topology — without it, K.2798 would appear as a top-level cluster anchor when it is actually a peripheral copy of a wider Mīs pî+Bīt-salāʾ-mê tradition.

### 3.3 The high-lift fuzzy pairs

Three candidates from the original K.2798 inspection (BM.35512, BM.45641, BM.34795) had fuzzy/exact lifts of 17×, 17×, 32×. eBL metadata identifies them:

| Pair | Identification |
|---|---|
| `BM.34795 ↔ BM.38295` (31× lift) | **Both are CANONICAL/Divination/Celestial/Enūma Anu Enlil/Sîn (EAE 1–22)** — two manuscript witnesses of the same lunar-omen subseries. Published independently (Reiner, Fs Borger CM 10, 294 and 297). |
| `BM.35512 ↔ K.2581` (17× lift) | BM.35512 = "Medical prescriptions; colophon"; K.2581 = `CANONICAL/Technical/Medicine/Therapeutic/Recipe/Prescription (Šumma amēlu)`. Two manuscripts of the same medical-recipe series, part of the exorcist's professional toolkit. |
| `BM.45641 ↔ BM.77056` (17× lift) | BM.45641 = Late Babylonian "Ritual and colophon. SE Antiochus III 1/7/1[]7?" — directly joins the BM.77056 magical-ritual cluster as a Hellenistic-period witness. |

**All three high-lift pairs are confirmed manuscript siblings of canonical compositions.** The exact-Jaccard method missed them because their orthographic divergence at individual positions broke too many trigrams, even though the underlying compositional identity was preserved.

#### Summary

- **7 of 11 are published, known compositions** that the methodology surfaced as isolates because their content is genuinely orthographically distinct from the rest of the corpus (lexical lists, bilingual proverbs, letters, MUL.APIN's astronomical vocabulary)
- **1 of 11 is an explicit missed join** (`BM.46372 → joined to 46338` per eBL's own catalog). The methodology should have caught this; it suggests BM.46338 may be excluded from the eBL indexed corpus or below a tokenization threshold
- **2 of 11 are genuinely under-cataloged** (K.6439, BM.35656) — candidate "previously-unknown composition" status, though both are very short fragments
- **1 of 11 (BM.48903 Egalkura) actually fits the BM.77056 cluster** — but its trigram pattern is sufficiently distinct from the cluster's core that even fuzzy-Jaccard at 0.20 doesn't catch it. Suggests the Egalkura series has divergent enough orthography to warrant its own sub-cluster.

**The actual "previously-unknown composition" yield from the v0.16 surface is 0–2 of 42 candidates** — well below the original spec target of 5/20. But the **manuscript-sibling-recovery yield is high**: 17 of 31 candidates rescued by fuzzy methods (55%), and recursive cluster expansion reveals integrated compositional families like the *āšipūtu* canon.

## 4. Discussion

### 4.1 What the pipeline does well

1. **Recovers wide-transmission manuscripts that exact methods miss.** The 0.30 trigram-Jaccard threshold is calibrated for nearby manuscripts but cannot reach peripheral witnesses of canonical compositions. Fuzzy 1-substitution recovery + recursive cluster expansion close this gap.
2. **Surfaces compositional unity at the corpus level.** The BM.77056 result demonstrates that the late-Mesopotamian exorcist library — a known scholarly canon — can be recovered as a single connected component by automated tooling, validating an Assyriological intuition with empirical precision.
3. **Distinguishes core witnesses from peripheral copies.** The K.2798 ↔ K.15325 result shows that BFS-topology metadata (depth, parent, fuzzy_J) identifies which cluster members are core and which are peripheral.

### 4.2 What the pipeline does NOT do

1. **Does not discover previously-unknown compositions** at a rate above ~5%. The eBL editors have already curated genuinely-novel material into other workflows; the bi-orphan surface mostly catches manuscript-sibling false-negatives + already-known short fragments.
2. **Does not handle very-short fragments well** (< 100 signs). The methodology's signal degrades sharply below this threshold; many short fragments are correctly flagged but provide no actionable inspection target.
3. **Does not recover joins that depend on metadata** (e.g., the `BM.46372 → 46338` case where the join is recorded in eBL's catalog but the linked tablet may be outside the indexed signs corpus).

### 4.3 What it means for future Assyriological tooling

The single largest improvement would be **stripping orthographic-variant noise at the tokenization level**, not at the matching level. The 2026-05-14 sign-variant normalization experiment found zero rank-improvement from collapse rules over 87 known siblings — but that experiment didn't test position-aware substitution at the trigram level, which is what the v0.17 fuzzy method effectively does. A pre-pass that canonicalizes known sign-form-variant pairs into a single token would yield equivalent results at exact-Jaccard with lower compute cost.

## 5. Reproducibility

All code in [github.com/Hugegreencandle/cuneiform-mcp](https://github.com/Hugegreencandle/cuneiform-mcp). Build sequence:

```bash
# 1. Fetch the eBL all-signs cache (~26 sec, ~33 MB)
node dist/index.js --prefetch

# 2. Build the corpus-viz lexical graph (~7 min)
cd ~/Desktop/corpus-viz && node build-graph.mjs

# 3. Build the v0.15 thematic embeddings (~4 min)
cd ~/Desktop/cuneiform-mcp && node scripts/build-embeddings.mjs

# 4. Build the v0.16 anomaly-surface joined index (~10 sec)
node scripts/build-anomaly-index.mjs

# 5. Reconstruct the BM.77056 cluster
echo '{"jsonrpc":"2.0","id":1,"method":"tools/call","params":{"name":"reconstruct_cluster","arguments":{"seed_tablet_id":"BM.77056","max_cluster_size":100,"max_depth":4}}}' | node dist/index.js
```

All probes shown above are deterministic (Random Indexing seed = 42, BFS frontier order = sorted by score within each depth). Re-running the pipeline reproduces the 100+ tablet BM.77056 cluster identically.

## 7. Three new claims this methods note offers

1. **`[my synthesis]`** The exact-trigram-Jaccard methodology that current cuneiform-discovery tooling defaults to (0.30 threshold) systematically under-recovers wide-transmission canonical compositions. The empirical demonstration: the BM.77056 cluster (the *āšipūtu* canon) is at least 100 manuscript witnesses across 20 museum prefixes, yet the canonical 0.30 method atomizes it into many disconnected sub-pairs because peripheral witnesses fall below threshold. Fuzzy 1-substitution Jaccard + BFS cluster reconstruction recover the underlying compositional unity.

2. **`[my synthesis]`** The late-Mesopotamian exorcist's library — long-recognized as a coherent canon from KAR 44 + colophon evidence (Lenzi 2008, Geller 2010, Maul 1994) — can be reconstructed empirically from pure-orthographic clustering at the sign-trigram level, without metadata or genre labels. This validates the textual-evidence consensus with computational precision and offers a methodology for recovering similar canon-level groupings in less-studied corpora.

3. **`[my synthesis]`** The bi-orphan methodology's real value is NOT discovering previously-unknown compositions (yield: ~5% of candidates) but rather identifying cataloging gaps + manuscript-sibling false-negatives in the lexical-overlap network (yield: ~55% of candidates rescued by fuzzy methods). This re-frames "anomaly detection" in cuneiform corpora as a tool for QA + recovery, not for novelty hunting — a more accurate framing of what the methodology actually delivers.
