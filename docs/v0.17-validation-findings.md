## Provenance-pattern observations

Of the 17 rescued pairs:
- **9 are within-prefix matches** (K↔K, BM↔BM, IM↔IM, YBC↔YBC) — same collection, same general provenance, but lexical-overlap was too localized to fire above 0.30
- **8 are cross-prefix matches** (K↔BM, BM↔YBC, K↔K.*, etc.) — cross-collection sibling pairs, even more interesting because they suggest text transmission across collection boundaries

`[my synthesis]` The cross-prefix fuzzy-sibling pairs (especially `BM.34795 ↔ BM.38295` at 31× lift, and `K.2798 ↔ Si.776` cross-collection at 2.7× lift) are the highest-value findings here — they suggest a composition was copied across different scribal traditions (Kuyunjik/Sippar/British Museum's later-acquired Babylonian material) with enough orthographic variation to defeat strict trigram-overlap methods. This is the kind of finding that v0.17's fuzzy method makes accessible at scale for the first time.
