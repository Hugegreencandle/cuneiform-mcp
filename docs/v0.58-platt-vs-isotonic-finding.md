# v0.58 Methodology Finding: Platt vs Isotonic on v0.30 Lacuna Fusion

Generated 2026-05-26 via `scripts/v0.58-isotonic-vs-platt-crossval.mjs`.

Triggered by cross-pollination from the wallet-fingerprint v0.6.2 work, which discovered isotonic regression dominates Platt scaling cleanly on near-step score distributions. The natural question: is cuneiform-mcp's v0.30 lacuna joint_score distribution near-step or sigmoid-shaped? And does that affect the v0.50 Platt calibration recommendation?

## TL;DR

- v0.30 lacuna fusion scores are **continuous-sigmoid-shaped**, not near-step.
- **Platt wins on ECE** (0.6374 → 0.0011, 587× lift) vs isotonic (0.0174, 37× lift).
- BUT Platt's "win" comes from **collapsing all predictions to the population prior** (0.183 ≈ top-1 accuracy 0.182). This is technically perfect aggregate calibration with **zero ranking discriminability**.
- The paper's 58× claim was the conservative (less-converged) Platt fit; this version achieves 587× by finding the degenerate-but-calibrated solution.
- v0.50 paper claim **stands** but warrants a footnote: Platt collapses to the prior because the underlying score has weak discriminability. Isotonic provides a less aggressive calibration that preserves the (limited) monotone signal.

## Detailed numbers (re-run, 500 samples)

| Method | ECE | Lift | Calibration character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Raw | 0.6374 | 1× | severely overconfident |
| Platt (refit) | 0.0011 | 587× | "amnesia calibration" — collapses to prior 0.183 |
| Isotonic | 0.0174 | 37× | preserves monotone trend; informative ranking retained |

The Platt fit produces A=0.0717, B=1.4369. With s in [0.5, 1.0]:
- s=0.5 → z = 0.5·0.0717 + 1.4369 = 1.473 → sigmoid(−1.473) = 0.187
- s=1.0 → z = 1.0·0.0717 + 1.4369 = 1.509 → sigmoid(−1.509) = 0.181

Output range: 0.181 to 0.187 — a 0.006-point spread over the entire input range. That's "essentially constant."

## Score distribution diagnostic

- Fraction in [0.0, 0.1): 0.0%
- Fraction in [0.9, 1.0): 22.8%
- Fraction in [0.1, 0.9): **77.2%**

This is a continuous (mid-mass-dominant) distribution — opposite of wallet-fingerprint's near-step distribution (80% at score=0). The score is meaningful but biased upward; Platt's sigmoid family is the appropriate functional form for re-mapping it.

## Recommendation for the paper

The v0.50 Platt claim "ECE 0.6374 → 0.0109 (58× improvement, well-calibrated)" is technically correct but invites misinterpretation. A revision would clarify:

> Platt scaling of v0.30 lacuna fusion scores achieves ECE 0.0011 (587× lift) by mapping the entire input range to the population prior (0.183 ≈ top-1 accuracy 0.182). This is calibration by collapsing to the prior, indicating that the underlying joint_score has weak discriminative power beyond reproducing the base rate. For applications requiring preserved ranking discriminability (e.g., active-learning candidate prioritization, top-K manuscript retrieval), isotonic regression provides a less aggressive recalibration (ECE 0.0174, 37× lift) that preserves the monotone signal. The choice between Platt and isotonic is task-dependent: Platt for aggregate calibration claims, isotonic for ranking-preserving recalibration.
