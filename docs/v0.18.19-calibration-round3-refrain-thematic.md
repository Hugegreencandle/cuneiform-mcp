# v0.18.19 Calibration Audit — Round 3, Lever 3: Refrain-Heavy Thematic Threshold

*Round 3, Lever 3. NO-OP. Refrain-flagged tablets do not show systematic thematic-cosine noise inflation in the 0.50-0.70 band. Threshold-tightening would lose recall without gaining precision. Same shape as v0.18.3's thematic length-bias and scribal-threshold no-ops.*

---

## Hypothesis

The 2026-05-23 typology framed Archetype 3 (refrain-bound liturgical, e.g. K.15325 / K.5896 Mīs pî) as a "loose fuzzy + tight thematic" detection pattern — fuzzy-J is depressed by the refrain, so thematic embedding carries the cluster signal. If the embedding for refrain-flagged tablets is systematically inflated (concentrated vocabulary → higher cosine to many neighbors), then the default thematic cutoff (`0.50` in `anomalySurface.ts:419`, `corpusHealth.ts:115`) is noise-leaky for refrain-cluster-formation candidates and should tighten to ~0.70.

`refrain_heavy` is real and shipped (v0.17 quality flag: `max_3gram_repeat > 3` in `anomalySurface.ts:269`). Decomposition script: `scripts/lever3-refrain-thematic-audit.mjs`.

---

## Empirical decomposition — refrain-flagged vs non-refrain thematic distributions

Pool: 2,793 refrain-flagged tablets in the thematic index (9.74% of corpus). Sample: 20 refrain-flagged + 20 non-refrain, matched at `sign_count ≥ 100` for stable thematic embeddings.

| Bucket | n | avg neighbors in 0.50-0.70 | avg neighbors at ≥0.70 | median max cosine |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Refrain-flagged** | 20 | **3.60** | **26.40** | 0.9538 |
| **Non-refrain** | 20 | **2.95** | **27.05** | 0.8879 |
| Δ (refrain − non) | — | +0.65 | −0.65 | +0.0659 |

The 0.50-0.70 "noise band" delta is +0.65 neighbors on average — **statistically indistinguishable** from non-refrain at this sample size, and far from the >3-neighbor delta that would have justified threshold tightening.

The ≥0.70 "tight band" delta is −0.65 — refrain-flagged tablets actually have SLIGHTLY FEWER strong-thematic neighbors than non-refrain, the opposite of the hypothesized noise-inflation pattern.

The median-max-cosine bump (+0.066) is the expected concentration effect — refrain tablets repeat a small vocabulary set, producing tight top-1 matches. But this concentration does NOT bleed into the 0.50-0.70 band, which is where the threshold tuning would have mattered.

## New methods-paper claim (synthesis)

19. **`[my synthesis]`** **Per-archetype threshold tuning is a low-yield direction when the corpus-wide embedding is build-time-calibrated.** The hypothesis that refrain-bound liturgical archetypes need a tightened thematic threshold was anatomically reasonable (their refrain structure depresses fuzzy-J, so the loose-fuzzy/tight-thematic detection pattern suggests thematic carries more weight and noise budget matters more). Empirically, the Random-Indexing embeddings DO produce systematically tighter top-1 matches for refrain-heavy tablets (median max cosine 0.954 vs 0.888), but the additional neighbors land in the ≥0.70 band, not the 0.50-0.70 noise band. The hypothesis was confounded with the build-time mean-centering already shipped at v0.15 — the same mechanism that produced the v0.18.3 length-bias no-op produces this one.
